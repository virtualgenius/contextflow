Why direction behaves differently per pattern

  • Directional patterns have an upstream and a downstream. Customer–supplier, conformist, and anti–corruption-layer all point from a consumer to the provider it relies on. "Depends on" walks toward the provider; "Depended on by" walks toward the consumers who would feel a change.
  • Symmetric patterns do not. Partnership and shared kernel are mutual commitments — neither side is upstream. So direction filtering traverses them both ways regardless of the setting. This is the behavior the two symmetric edges here let you confirm.
  • "Depends on" answers planning questions. From a team, it shows the blast-in radius: everything this team would need stable to ship. "Depended on by" shows the blast-out radius: who you'd have to coordinate with before changing your contracts.
  • Unassigned / external contexts (Stripe, Carriers, Tax, Fraud) render with a dashed boundary. They still participate in adjacency — an external dependency is exactly the kind of thing a focused view should surface.
  • Still one model. Every node and edge is the same ACME project; the focus is a view-time computation. Saving a configuration (e.g. "Fulfillment + depends-on, 2 hops") is the C4-style named lens, deliberately left out here so we settle the interaction first.
