Adjacency Filter — Focused View on One Map

Non-destructive focus for large maps. Pick a focus (a whole team, or click a single context), choose how many relationship hops to include, and everything outside that neighborhood fades back — the map keeps its shape and spatial memory, you just stop having to read all of it at once. This is the "show me everything adjacent to this team" interaction, on the single connected model rather than separate boards.

Try it: set Focus on team → Fulfillment with 1 hop — you get the Fulfillment contexts plus only what directly touches them. Drag the Adjacency slider to 0 (just the team), 2, then 3 to watch the neighborhood grow. Or click any single context to focus on it alone. Flip Direction to "Depends on" vs "Depended on by" to follow relationships upstream or downstream only. Toggle Dim vs Hide to feel the open design question: dim preserves the map you've learned; hide reclaims the space.

What this is exercising

  • One connected model, many lenses. Nothing is duplicated or moved. The focus is a view-time computation over the existing contexts and relationships, so it can't drift out of sync the way a second map would.
  • Seed set. A team focus seeds the neighborhood with every context owned by that team; a single-context focus seeds it with just that one. Adjacency then grows outward by relationship hops (breadth-first).
  • Direction is the DDD-meaningful refinement. "Depends on" walks outgoing relationships (what this neighborhood consumes / is downstream of); "Depended on by" walks incoming (who would feel a change here). "Both" ignores direction and is the plain "what's near this team" answer.
  • Dim vs Hide is the call to make. Dim (0 hops still readable in context) keeps spatial memory and shows the "edge" of the neighborhood; Hide declutters hardest for a 50+ context map but loses the surrounding shape. Both are one line of rendering difference.
  • Edges follow their endpoints. A relationship is full-strength only when both ends are in the neighborhood, so the boundary of the focus reads cleanly.
  • Natural next step (not shown): a focus configuration like this is exactly what a saved named view ("Payments neighbourhood") would persist, giving the C4-style "jump around" between scoped lenses.
